The Case of The Broken Shot Glass

It was march 25th, on the way back from a trip around the northwest. Mason was so happy with everything he has accomplished in the last 7 days. Spent time with his closes friends he got to see some of the most magnificent places he has ever witnessed.

After visiting each state, he was in he thought the best way to hold on to the memories was to collect a shot glass from that state that signified the status of the trip. The one shot glass that stood out the most was oddly enough from Idaho. Idaho was the beginning and end of the trip and held up as the start to a great week and the perfect ending to it. The shot glass was clear with a graphic of a cartoon bear.

The bear was holding up a sign that ironically said look out for bears. It was a funny image that always brought a smile to Mason. Inside the shot glass however, was the real reason he bought the glass. It was a little plastic figure of a bear. The outside of the glass said save the bear, which was a little context to drinking the shot. Every time he took a shot with his friends he always choose that glass because to him it was the best one.

7 days of having the best memories and he finally came home. After a long trip, it took a couple of days to get everything he brought clean. It finally came time to cleaning the glassware he bought on the trip. Mason was running low on time for he had to go to work. He left it out hoping that someone will clean it for him.

He placed it in the lower rack where everyone knows that is where you put the dirtiest dishes because it has the most water to wash it. At the end of the wash another friend pulled it out knowing the history of it and saw that the bear, has broken off from the glass.

Feeling sad himself he told mason and both wondered who would do such a thing to a glass that meant so much. Not knowing who loaded the dish washer they wondered endlessly. The take away they want to have people know was to always check what you are putting in the dishwasher is ok to go in it. If not hand wash it, please.
